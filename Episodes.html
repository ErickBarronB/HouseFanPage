Featured Episodes

Pilot (Season 1, Episode 1)

Original Air Date: November 16, 2004

Directed by: Bryan Singer

Written by: David Shore

Summary: The series opens with Dr. Gregory House being introduced to a new case involving a kindergarten teacher who collapses during class. The patient, Rebecca Adler, presents with symptoms that baffle her doctors. House, despite his reluctance to take on new patients, becomes intrigued by the medical mystery. As he investigates, we see his unconventional diagnostic methods and his team of three fellows: Dr. Allison Cameron, Dr. Robert Chase, and Dr. Eric Foreman. The episode establishes House's character as a brilliant but abrasive diagnostician who solves cases through his unique perspective and willingness to break rules.

Key Moments: House's first meeting with Dr. Lisa Cuddy, the introduction of his diagnostic team, and the revelation of his chronic leg pain and Vicodin addiction.

Three Stories (Season 1, Episode 21)

Original Air Date: May 17, 2005

Directed by: Paris Barclay

Written by: David Shore

Summary: This landmark episode is told through three interconnected stories that House presents to his medical students. The first story follows a patient with a mysterious illness, the second reveals House's own medical history and how he lost the use of his leg, and the third explores the emotional toll of his condition. The episode is notable for its innovative narrative structure, where House's personal story of his leg infarction and subsequent addiction to painkillers is revealed through flashbacks. This episode provides crucial backstory for House's character and explains his physical limitations and dependency on Vicodin.

Key Moments: House's leg infarction flashback, the revelation of his pain management struggles, and the emotional impact of his disability on his life and career.

These episodes showcase the series' innovative storytelling and character development.
